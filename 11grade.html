The Proposal Project

Throughout my 11th grade year I didn't work on lot of projects due to covid-19. Since our school is about Project Based Learning, I found it much harder this year compared to my other years. We ended up not collaborating in our classes as much which was a struggle for me. Though I found a way out and saw how I grew in the aspect of collaboration. Since we came back in the 4th quarter I was able to communicate and collaborate on a project with my peers. The project that we worked on was the Proposal Project. The Proposal Project was to create an idea or proposal to give to the school and figure out how it can help our school. This porject was in our English class, so the main concept was about the writing and the experience of a proposal writer. We ended up getting to know a special speaker in the beginning. (Her name.) was a proposal writer for a company. She taught us the struggles and how to overcome them when writing. The biggest problem that she came across was working together with people. The main thing to fix that was to always keep on top of everything and let the group know. I ended up using these skills to my advantage when working with my peers. We constantly kept asking each other if we needed help and if there was any problems we could fix together. In our log books we would tell each other what we completed that day and how we felt about the situation. Overall it was a good succeses because we ended up getting a really good grade on the project.

In my Fitness assesment class, we did a project called the Medical Career Project. My group and I ended up researching Pharmicists because no other group did so. Since the project was coming to end I realized that I had spent to much time messing around when I should have been working on the project. Since the project was about researching, the requirements for the presentation was to have 30 words or less on each slide with a maximum of 10 slides. It was hard to remember all the information in such a little time. Trying to get all the information into the slides was difficult, but ended up doing alright. Next thing you know we were going 2nd on the presentations. We were worried that are work wouldn't be accepted and my team was going crazy.
